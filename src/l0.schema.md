# L0 — Intake schema & record format

The canonical datapoint set the **L0 intake/interview layer** elicits to feed the **L1 Diagnostic
Engine**. The adaptive interviewer (`src/l0.template.md` → `dist/l0.system.md`) reuses the shared
kernel's five axes and Conditional Activation Map, so it interviews for exactly what L1 classifies
on — they cannot drift (same `src/kernel.md`, same `kernel-sha256`). This file is the human-readable
contract, a fill-in "floor mode" form for when a chat interview isn't wanted, and the spec for the
private dataset record. It is **documentation — not compiled** (only `l0.template.md` is built).

## Principles
- **Classify before asking.** Infer the five axes (Buyer, Sales Motion, Ticket/Cycle, Offer
  Character, Stage) from whatever's given; ask only the load-bearing fields still missing. Never ask
  a procurement shop about ad-CAC.
- **Motion-neutral.** Never assume a sales motion. "Conversion" = whatever turns interest into a paid
  customer for *this* motion (a sales close, a checkout, an opt-in → purchase, a bid win). Use
  "transaction value," not "deal size" / "booked job."
- **Stage-conditional rigor.** A pre-revenue / proof-stage business has no acquisition numbers — do
  not demand them; accept proof signals (waitlist, pre-orders, beta users, testimonials, LOIs).
- **Numbers carry confidence.** Tag each figure `measured | estimated | guessed`.
- **Preserve the operator's lexicon verbatim.**

## Fields
**R** = required · **O** = optional · **R\*** = at least one in the group. **Floor mode** = the
required fields + the symptom.

### Group 1 — Frame (types the business)
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| What you sell | R | offer character; lexicon |
| Who buys it (the real decision-maker) | R | buyer axis |
| Price / typical transaction value | R | ticket axis; monetization |
| How you get customers today | R | sales-motion axis (the single most load-bearing field) |
| Stage + rough monthly revenue | R | stage axis |

### Group 2 — Numbers (turns advice into a numbered prescription)
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| Volume in (leads / visitors / bids) | R\* | acquisition |
| Conversion rate (motion-appropriate) | R\* | acquisition |
| Cost to acquire (per lead / per customer) | R\* | acquisition; monetization |
| ≥1 hard acquisition number | R *(waived pre-revenue → proof signal)* | keeps the diagnosis off a guess |
| Margin · repeat / retention · transaction value | O | monetization |
| Per-channel unit economics (CAC / conversion / volume / value) | O | finds the real lever vs a blended average |
| Owned-audience asset (list size / following / content cadence) | O | the acquisition asset for info/DTC |
| Product outcome (completion / results / refund / return rate) | O | value-equation "perceived likelihood" for info/e-com |

### Group 3 — The loss (diagnosis-changing)
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| Of the sales you LOSE, why? — the **loss reasons** split (price / trust / timing / availability / no-decision) | O (strongly probed) | binding-constraint selection — separates "not enough leads" from "we don't close" |

### Group 4 — What you've tried & **why you stopped** (highest-value qualitative)
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| Per prior offer / price / channel / positioning: hypothesis → what happened → **why you stopped** (structural failure / abandoned-too-early / poor execution / wrong timing / lost interest) | O (strongly probed) | prevents re-prescribing an abandoned *winner* — a lever that "didn't sell" is often a packaging failure, not a dead concept |

### Group 5 — Competition & the wedge
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| Who you lose to + their pricing posture | O | offer character |
| **How they cut corners / underdeliver** | O (probed) | the differentiation wedge / risk seeds-of-doubt |
| Their guarantee / risk terms; why a customer honestly picks them over you today | O | the real constraint |

### Group 6 — Real vs perceived differentiation
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| What you SAY makes you different | O | constraint |
| What you **can prove** (mechanism / proof / unfair advantage a third party would verify) | O | monetization |
| Would the customer agree it's real? | O | constraint — the gap between stated and provable is frequently the diagnosis |

### Group 7 — Referral / partner structure
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| Who already **refers you** | O | distribution / lead-getters |
| What the partner actually wants (status / reciprocity / money) | O | the channel mechanism |
| The partner's structural leverage (one partner ⇒ many buyers) | O | the multiplier lever |

### Group 8 — The operator & the why (acceptability constraint)
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| **Personal goal** / target (+ a number) | O (probed) | a plan that violates the why is a wrong answer regardless of the math |
| Time horizon · risk tolerance | O | which plays are fundable |
| Constraints won't cross (won't hire / won't sell / won't run ads) | O | bounds the prescription space |

### Group 9 — Situation & sharpening
| Field | R/O | Feeds |
|---|---|---|
| The symptom — where it hurts | R (floor) | binding constraint |
| What "fixed" looks like in ~90 days (a number) | O | verdict stakes |
| Capacity/fulfillment ceiling · geography/seasonality · regulatory angle · cash/runway | O *(triage-gated)* | gates whether acquisition is even the right lever |

## Private dataset record format
One record per completed interview, written by the interviewer (or hand-filled from this form).
**Records live under `datasets/intake/` and are git-ignored — never committed.** Only this *schema*
is versioned (code/spec in git, data out — same discipline as `eval-results*.json`).

```yaml
schema_version: 1
kernel_sha256: <stamp matching the engine version that produced/consumed this>   # replay correctness
interview_date: <YYYY-MM-DD>          # passed in — the engine does not invent dates
source: human | l0.system.md
business_id: <name / id>              # PII — stripped on anonymize
inferred_axes:                        # mark each as stated vs inferred
  buyer: ...
  motion: ...
  ticket: ...
  offer_character: ...
  stage: ...
raw_answers:
  <field>: { value: ..., confidence: measured | estimated | guessed }
business_context_blob: |              # the EXACT text handed to L1 (the load-bearing artifact)
  ...
# optional, only if the run was executed / graded:
l1_diagnosis: ...
grade: ...
notes: ...
```

**Flywheel.** Anonymize a record (planned `tools/anonymize.py` — strip Group-1 PII, optional number
rounding, keep `inferred_axes` + blob) and it converts directly into a golden case for
`evals/tests/l1_cases.yaml` (which keys on a `business_context` + an axis-classification rubric).
Real interviews become regression fixtures that defend the agnosticism claim over time. Keep the
record → case conversion a manual one-shot for now (YAGNI — no auto-promotion pipeline).

> **Replay note (honest):** the `kernel_sha256` stamp lets you confirm the **axes** reproduce on a
> matching engine version — not that a full LLM diagnosis reproduces verbatim (those vary run-to-run).
